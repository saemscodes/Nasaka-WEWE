Nasaka WEWE is a secure messaging app built for Kenyan activists, journalists, civic educators and anyone who needs a safe, resilient way to communicate — especially during internet blackouts. Unlike traditional messaging apps, Nasaka WEWE doesn't rely on a central server — messages are synchronized directly between users' devices. If the Internet's down, Nasaka WEWE can sync via Bluetooth, Wi-Fi or memory cards, keeping information flowing in a crisis. If the Internet's up, Nasaka WEWE can sync via the Tor network, protecting users and their relationships from surveillance.

The app features private messages, private groups, forums and blogs. All communication is end-to-end encrypted. Support for the Tor network is built into the app. Everything you do in Nasaka WEWE is only stored on your device unless you decide to share it with other users.

Nasaka WEWE supports dual-mode authentication: sign in with your CEKA (Civic Education Kenya) account when online, or create a local offline account with just a name and PIN — no internet required. When connectivity returns, you can optionally link your local account to your CEKA membership.

There are no advertisements and no tracking. The source code is completely open for anyone to inspect. Nasaka WEWE is built upon the battle-tested Briar encrypted messaging engine, with civic engagement features by Civic Education Kenya.
